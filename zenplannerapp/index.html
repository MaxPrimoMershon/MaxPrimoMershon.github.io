Strengths

  • Visualize & Communicate the Big Picture

  • Story

    Project team was all over the place. People were making uniformed decisions because they weren't aware of how their decisions impacted the bigger picture. Inspired by a previous presentation given by the UX leads, I created a walkthrough of the app and presented it project leadership.

  • Why a fit?

    Product managers need to be able to see the bigger picture, so they can put together all of the pieces appropriately.

  • Accomodate Range of Perspectives

  • Story

    Lead requirement and use case development sessions to create an application that was user friendly, technically feasible given project constrains, and user friendly. The requirements and use cases needed to get approval from business, app/tech arch, design/dev, test and UX teams.

  • Why a fit?

    Product managers are the bridge between design, business and technical teams. THey must be able to make sure all teams work together to achieve the product goal as it fits into the overall company's goals.

  • Results Driven Process

  • Story

    After the business requirements were handed of to the techincal teams, a lot of changes were forced to be made. Created analytics to see where in the requirements development process things were going wrong. Found trends and created an action plan based on the findings.

  • Why a fit?

    Business, especially when it comes to the fitness business, is about results. If the customer isn't seeing results, then there is no reason for the business to exist. Processes tailored to the goals of a project is a great way to get stuff done in an organized and timely fashion.

  • Customer Focused

  • Story

    From reffing hockey when I was 13 years old, to caddying during the summers when in college, to presenting deliverables to clients of Fortune 100 companies, it is important to always keep the customer in mind. That means working with coaches and players to control the situation during high intensity hockey games, tailoring your caddy job to golfers from the 70 year old grandma to corporate execs, and understanding your customer's goal in order to create systematic processes to deliver valye more efficiently.

  • Why a fit?

    Zen Planner is incredibly customer focused. They make sure the customer is contacted after signing up to see if they need help. They have developed a passionate and loyal customer base that has contributed termendously to their success.
